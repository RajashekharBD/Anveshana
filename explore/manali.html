About Manali

Nestled in the breathtaking Himalayas, Manali is a picturesque hill station in Himachal Pradesh that offers stunning landscapes, thrilling adventure sports, and a peaceful retreat from city life. Famous for its snow-capped mountains, apple orchards, and gushing Beas River, Manali is a year-round destination that transforms with each season - from snowy winters perfect for skiing to cool summers ideal for trekking and paragliding. The town also serves as a gateway to the remote Lahaul and Spiti valleys.

Why Visit Manali?

Mountain Views

Marvel at the majestic snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas from Rohtang Pass, Solang Valley, and various viewpoints around Manali.

Adventure Sports

Experience thrilling activities like paragliding, skiing, river rafting, zorbing, and trekking in the Himalayas.

Hot Springs

Relax in the natural hot water springs at Vashisht village, known for their therapeutic properties and ancient temples.

Trekking Routes

Explore famous treks like Hampta Pass, Beas Kund, and Bhrigu Lake that start from Manali and offer spectacular views.

Spiritual Sites

Visit ancient temples like Hadimba Devi Temple, Manu Temple, and Tibetan monasteries in the nearby town of Naggar.

Local Cuisine

Savor Himachali specialties like Sidu, Babru, Trout fish, and local apple products including jams and ciders.
